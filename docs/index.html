Incremental Disentanglement for Environment-Aware Zero-Shot Text-to-Speech Synthesis

Ye-Xin Lu, Hui-Peng Du, Zheng-Yan Sheng, Yang Ai, Zhen-Hua Ling,
National Engineering Research Center of Speech and Language Information Processing
University of Science and Technology of China
Code Paper

Abstract

This paper proposes IDEA-TTS, an Incremental Disentanglement-based Environment-Aware zero-shot text-to-speech (TTS) method that can synthesize speech for new speakers while possessing the acoustic characterization from a reference environmental speech. Built upon the VITS framework, IDEA-TTS employs an environment-robust speaker encoder to extract robust speaker embeddings from an environmental speech for zero-shot TTS. To effectively disentangle the environment, speaker, and text information, we propose an incremental disentanglement process, where a spectrogram enhancer is designed to first disentangle the environmental spectrogram into an enhanced spectrogram and an environment mask. The environment mask is processed by an environment encoder to extract environmental embeddings, while the enhanced spectrogram facilitates the further disentanglement of the speaker and text information. Finally, both the speaker embedding and environment embedding are conditioned into the decoder for environmental speech generation. Experimental results demonstrate that our proposed IDEA-TTS achieves promising environment-aware TTS performance in terms of speech quality, speaker similarity, and environmental similarity. Additionally, it also achieves state-of-the-art performance in the acoustic environment conversion task.
